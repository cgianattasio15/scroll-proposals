Section 01

Where you are right now.

You're Cincinnati's Top Doc four years running. Vogue. People. Marie Claire. Board-certified MD, FAAD. Marie Claire Beauty Awards 2026 judge.

Your Instagram audience is 1,184 people.

That gap is not a content problem. It's a positioning gap. The credential ceiling you've earned is roughly ten times the size of the audience that currently knows about it. The opportunity is not to invent a new format. It's to organize and scale what you've already shown you can do (the at-home chemical peel reel, the microneedling POV, the Marie Claire announcement) and surround it with the authority content the credential actually unlocks.

Section 02

Why now.

The most common reservation founders have when a major build is coming: should I wait until the new space is ready before investing in social?

It's a reasonable question. It's also the most expensive answer to get wrong.

If you wait

Mason opens to the audience you have today. You spend the first six months after opening trying to do social media catch-up while running a brand new location. The single most valuable content arc available to you (a real building going up in real time) is gone.

If you start now

Two things compound. Kenwood keeps converting. And every week of construction is a week of anticipation content. Groundbreaking. Framing. Finishes. Walk-throughs. Reveal. Build-in-public is one of the highest-performing organic formats on Instagram. By the time Mason opens, you're opening to an audience that's been watching you build and decided weeks ago they're booking.

Same dollars, very different outcome. That's the thesis. The rest of this document is how we execute against it.

The two Mason narratives

One narrative anchors every piece of Mason content for the next six months. You pick. We build.

Option A

Same Mona. Same care. Bigger home.

Continuity-led. The audience that loves Kenwood follows you to Mason because the care doesn't change. The space gets bigger; the relationship deepens. Reassuring for existing patients, inviting for new ones who've heard of you.

Option B

New chapter, elevated experience.

Reveal-led. Mason is a moment. A bigger studio, an expanded service set, a step change in what's possible inside Cincinnati Skin Center. The build-in-public arc carries weight because something genuinely new is being unveiled at the end.
