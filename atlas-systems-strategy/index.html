Section 3Performance · Measurement

We track the signals that predict customers.

Followers and views are context, not the scoreboard. For a business with your deal size, the metric that matters is qualified fit calls. Everything else tells us whether we're on the path to them.

Top of funnel
  • Reach to new accounts
  • Sends, the top 2026 discovery signal
  • Saves, the authority signal
  • Is the right audience finding us?
Middle of funnel
  • Saves on frameworks and how-tos
  • Watch time, the strongest attention and trust signal
  • Profile visits, interest beyond the post
  • Are they coming to trust the founder?
Bottom of funnel
  • DMs and qualified conversations
  • Link taps to the booking path
  • Booked fit calls, the north star
  • Are they converting?
How the score works. Each month's content is scored on a weighted framework where saves and click-through carry the most weight, because they best predict customer behavior. You get one clear score, your top posts, and the focus for next month.
The conversion lens

Right audience. Clear path. The right metrics. Get Atlas in front of the right owners. Walk them through a clear path to a fit call. Track the metrics aligned to that path. With those three working, qualified lead generation follows. Hard last-mile attribution isn't running yet. We track signal first, attribution-grade second.

Appendix

Posting cadence, days, and timing.

The reference behind our cadence. These are starting defaults we calibrate to Atlas's own data once posts are live.

How often

3 feed posts + 2 story days

Monday, Wednesday, Friday on the feed, one per funnel stage, with Tuesday and Thursday for stories and reactive moments. Consistency beats volume.

What days

Weekdays for a B2B owner

Your buyer is an operator checking their phone between meetings. Tuesday through Thursday tend to perform best for reach to business owners. We confirm against real data.

What time

Early morning and lunch

Around 7 to 9am and the midday break are when this audience scrolls. We post ahead of those windows and adjust to Atlas's actual top times.

Format mix

40 / 35 / 25

Roughly 40% top of funnel, 35% middle, 25% bottom. Read monthly, not post by post. Reels for reach, carousels for saves, stories for conversion.

Engagement

About 30 minutes a day

Manual engagement with the right local accounts, and replying to every comment and DM. The algorithm rewards it and your buyer notices it.

Distribution

Cross-post to LinkedIn

The founder's strongest content also runs on LinkedIn 3 to 5 times a week, where many of these owners also spend time.
