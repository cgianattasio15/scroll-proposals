What We Know

Three priorities. One system.

Everything in this proposal maps to what you and Allison discussed and what we observed across your account and credentials. Three priorities — top, middle, and bottom of the funnel — shape every strategic decision.

01

Further establish your brand awareness

The agency lens. Top-of-funnel awareness is about getting your brand in front of the audience that should already know about it. Reach is the wedge. We use organic content to expand the audience you can talk to before any conversion conversation happens.

Applied here. You've built a credentialed practice with real authority — Top Doc four years running, Vogue, People, Marie Claire, MD-led aesthetics. The audience that knows about it is 1,184 people. The audience that should know about it is much larger. We get you in front of the Cincinnati audience that's currently scrolling past nurse-led med spas because nobody has shown them the alternative.

TOFU · Awareness
02

Build authority and engagement that earns trust

The agency lens. Middle-of-funnel is about earning the consideration. Education, expertise, and engagement turn awareness into trust. The audience starts to see you as the credible choice, not just a familiar name.

Applied here. Cincinnati has plenty of med spas. What it doesn't have is a clear story of why MD-led, board-certified dermatology delivers a different outcome. Education content, treatment walkthroughs, founder-led demos (the at-home chemical peel and microneedling formats you've already proven work) — these are what make Cincinnati Skin Center the obvious choice when someone is comparing options.

MOFU · Authority
03

Convert awareness into booked patients and a Mason waitlist

The agency lens. Bottom-of-funnel is where attention becomes action. Calls-to-action, lead capture, and conversion infrastructure turn engaged followers into booked customers.

Applied here. Every piece of content needs a job. The highest-priority conversion targets are Kenwood bookings (today's revenue) and the Mason waitlist (tomorrow's revenue). Content drives both, with CTAs and lead-capture mechanics that match the audience and the moment.

BOFU · Conversion

Unique to Your Engagement

The Mason runway. A 6-month anticipation arc.

You're not just opening a new office. You're building a public anticipation arc that turns construction itself into content. This is the strategic feature of the engagement and the single biggest leverage point we have between now and Mason open.

1

Pre-build · Months 0–1 — Foundation & Tease

Audience build kicks off with the announcement that Mason is coming. Voice: confident, intentional, brand-consistent. Content: founder-led explanation of why a new location, what Mason patients will get, when to expect it. CTA: join the Mason waitlist (we build a simple capture mechanic via link-in-bio).

2

Groundbreaking & Framing · Months 1–3 — Build Reveal

Major construction milestones become content moments. Groundbreaking event reel. First-walls-up reel. Time-lapse content. Founder walk-throughs showing the space coming together. Voice: behind-the-scenes intimacy, build-in-public authenticity. Tier 1 metric: reach plus shares plus saves (waitlist conversion follows).

3

Finishes & Design · Months 3–5 — Curation & Story

Interior design choices, equipment additions, treatment-room reveals. Voice: aspirational, design-forward. This is where the Mason brand identity question (same-brand vs. evolved-brand, locked at kickoff) plays out visually. Content gets richer as the space gets closer to ready. Waitlist push intensifies in Month 5.

4

Pre-launch · 4–6 weeks before open — Waitlist Sprint

Concentrated waitlist conversion push. Founder POV: "Here's what's about to be possible at Mason." Treatment menu teases. Booking-window announcements for Mason patients first. CTA: claim a Mason booking slot.

5

Open · Mason grand-open moment — Celebration & Conversion

Grand-open content event. Walk-through reveal. Founder welcome. Waitlist patients booked first. Visual celebration moment that becomes the Mason highlight on the account permanently.

6

Post-open · Ongoing — Two Locations, One Brand

Content continues to serve both Kenwood and Mason patients. Location-tagged content. Patient stories at both locations. The Mason arc becomes a permanent reference point in the account's narrative.

Why this matters. Build-in-public is one of the highest-performing organic formats on Instagram. Most practices opening new locations miss this leverage entirely because they wait to "have something to show." You don't need a finished space to start. The build itself is the story. By the time Mason opens, you're not opening to a quiet launch — you're opening to an audience that has watched the space go up and decided weeks ago they're booking.
