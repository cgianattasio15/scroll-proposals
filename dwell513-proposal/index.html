04The hero franchise⭐ Signature SeriesWalkthrough Reel · recurringMOFU · Authority
What This House Is Hiding
Doug walks a home and names what you cannot see. Cosmetic, structural, or the thirty-thousand-dollar problem arriving in three years. This is the franchise only DWELL513 can make, and it is the reason people will follow this account.
▼

The engine. Every episode, a new house. Doug walks in cold, finds three things, and grades each one on camera. The structure never changes, so the audience learns the format and comes back for the verdict.

Why it is unrepeatable. A person who physically restored historic homes for a living, saying true, specific, slightly uncomfortable things about a house, cannot be imitated by an agent who has only ever sold them. Every competitor can talk about a house. Doug can read one.

Hook options.

  • "I built and restored houses before I ever sold one. Here's what this room is telling me."
  • "This kitchen is beautiful. It's also hiding a thirty-thousand-dollar problem."
  • "Three things in this house. One is cosmetic. One is structural. One I'd walk away from."

(If Doug confirms the number of years he spent building, restore the specificity: a real figure in the hook is stronger than the general claim. Do not invent one.)

On-screen text. A running verdict stamp per finding. The stamp is the visual signature of the franchise.

Cosmetic Structural Walk Away

Caption direction. Open with the finding, not the credential. Name the material and the number. Close with a genuine question to the audience, never a pitch.

Why it travels. It is insider expertise the viewer can immediately apply to their own house, which is the saveable, sendable combination. Specificity is the engine. "Knob-and-tube wiring" and "thirty thousand dollars" travel. "Old electrical" and "expensive problems" do not.

Strategic reach. Local collaborations, proactive outreach, and community events, the moves that spike an account into the algorithm.

How we measureSignal first, qualified leads second
▼

A monthly performance report, first week of every month, scored against your goals, with your top posts and next month's focus, plus biweekly check-ins. We track signal first and qualified leads second. We do not promise last-click attribution that organic social cannot honestly deliver.

Social media is a two-year game. That is not a hedge, it is the actual shape of the thing.

Months 1 through 3 are foundation and testing. We are learning what your specific audience responds to, and you should expect to see reach move before anything else does. The rest of year one builds the baseline: a bigger, better-qualified audience, and trust that compounds every time you show up. Year two is when the interest turns into inquiries at a rate that feels like a machine, because two years of proof is sitting behind every post.

There are no steps to skip here. Any partner who promises you leads in thirty days is selling you something. Commit to the process, think in years rather than weeks, and the results are real.
