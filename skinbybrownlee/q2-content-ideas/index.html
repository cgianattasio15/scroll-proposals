Metric 01
Retention %
The percentage of a Reel watched before drop-off. SBB sits at 24% against a 65–80% Rise-stage target. This is a hook and opening architecture problem, not a topic problem — March's top Reel hit 61.3%, confirming the audience will stay when the opening delivers. Every idea below is built around the first 1.5 seconds and visual momentum through to the end.
What drives high retention
1.5s
The decision window
50% of viewers drop off in the first three seconds. The hook must create immediate curiosity, urgency, or a pattern interrupt before the caption overlay even appears.
7–20s
Optimal length
Reels under 30 seconds achieve the highest completion rates. Short enough to loop, long enough to deliver one sharp idea. Zero filler — every second must earn its place.
3s
Cut frequency
Jump cuts every 1.5–3 seconds average 32% higher engagement than single-shot video. Static shots beyond 4 seconds are exit points. Visual momentum is non-negotiable.

Key principles: Start mid-action, never mid-introduction. Layer on-screen text as a second hook — half of Instagram video is watched on mute. No slow pans, no "hey guys," no context-setting before the point.

The Villain Reveal
RetentionShares Proven Format Reel · 10–15 secApril
Format origin: The "this product is hurting you" cold open is one of the highest-performing retention hooks in the skincare and wellness space — proven across dermatology creators, supplement brands, and e-commerce beauty accounts. The pattern: hold up something the audience owns → challenge its safety → reveal the better alternative. Adapted here for April's Dark Spot Issue theme.

Execution: Cold open — Sylvia holds up a recognizable drugstore brightening product or popular SPF. On-screen text: "This is making your dark spots worse." No intro, no warmup. The viewer immediately needs to know if they own this product. Three seconds in she pivots: here's what's actually in it and why it's failing you — lands on Toned Brightening Serum or Radiant A Retinol as the contrast. The fear of being wrong about something in their bathroom is the retention engine. Shareable because women will send this to a friend who owns the same product.
Hook options — test all three
"The SPF you're wearing is making your dark spots worse."
"Most brightening serums don't have enough niacinamide to actually do anything."
"This ingredient is in half your skincare. It's the reason dark spots keep coming back."

The Live Product Audit
RetentionShares Pattern Break Reel · 20–30 secJune
Why this is a Pattern Break: Ingredient label reads exist in skincare content — but they're almost always slow, educational, and framed as information delivery. Nobody has turned this into a high-stakes, real-time format where the product either passes or fails live on camera. The tension of "will it pass?" turns a skincare lesson into a suspense arc — which is the retention mechanism.

Execution: Sylvia holds up the Brightening Peel Pads (June launch). On-screen text: "I'm reading this label live. Here's what I'm looking for." She flips it over, reads the ingredients fast, calls out what she's checking — specific, no script, real time. Product passes. "That's why I made this." Under 30 seconds. The live audit works especially well for a product launch because it creates first-impression credibility — this isn't a polished ad, it's Sylvia's expert eye in real time. Audience can't scroll because they need to see if the product passes.
Hook options — test all three
"I'm reading this label live. Let's see if it's actually worth it."
"New launch. I'm auditing it in real time — here's what I'm looking for."
"I only recommend products that pass this test. Does this one?"

Metric 02
Saves
A save means the viewer plans to act on this content later — the strongest pre-purchase intent signal on the platform. SBB is at 152 saves per month against a 300 Rise-stage floor, and the number has been flat for two months. The fix is format and framing — carousels generate 2–3× more saves than Reels, and content needs to function as a reference tool, not just education.
What drives saves
8–12
Carousel slide count
Educational carousels with 8–12 slides consistently outperform shorter versions — they deliver enough depth to earn the bookmark. Every slide must add value. Filler kills completion rate and saves.
2–3×
Carousels vs. Reels
Carousels generate 2–3× more saves than Reels. The swipe mechanic creates dwell time and micro-commitments that video can't replicate. For save-first content, carousel is the format.
Utility
The save trigger
People save content they'll act on: routines, checklists, ingredient guides, diagnostic tools. The test: would someone screenshot this at the bathroom mirror or before placing an order?

Key principles: First slide names the value explicitly before the swipe. Last slide closes with a direct CTA — "Save this for later" works. Structure every carousel: problem → insight → steps → proof → CTA.

The Peel Pad Owner's Manual
Saves Pattern Break Carousel · 10 slidesJune
Why this is a Pattern Break: Product launch content in skincare almost always follows two formats: "look at our new product" announcements, or before/after results posts. Neither earns saves. An "owner's manual" for a new product is essentially unused in this space — but it positions the carousel as the thing you reference every time you use the product, not something you consume once and scroll past. People save it before the product even arrives.

Execution: Framed as a literal owner's manual for the Brightening Peel Pads. Cover slide: "The Brightening Peel Pads Owner's Manual — save this before you open the box." Slides cover: what the pads actually do, who they're for, who should avoid them, frequency of use, what to layer with them, what not to layer, what to expect week 1 vs. week 4, and a one-page quick-reference summary at the end. This format earns saves before the viewer uses the product — and creates a functional reason to purchase: you want the manual so you can use it correctly.

Metric 03
Shares
A DM send is the highest-weighted distribution signal in Instagram's 2026 algorithm — it tells the platform the content sparked a real connection between people. SBB is at 71 shares per month against a 200 Rise-stage floor. Shares require a fundamentally different content architecture than saves. The goal is the "I need to send this to someone" reaction. Design for the forward, not the like.
What drives shares
DM
DM sends are #1
Instagram weights DM sends as the strongest distribution signal for Reels. Content built for the private "this reminded me of you" send — not a public tag — is the highest-value share action on the platform.
Feel
3 share triggers
The three types of content that get shared most: something relatable that validates a shared experience, something genuinely funny, or a strong opinion that makes someone think "I need to know if my friend agrees with this."
Short
Under 15s for shares
Shareable Reels are typically 7–15 seconds — short enough to watch in a group chat without context. The lower the friction to watch, the more likely someone forwards it.

Key principles: Ask "who would someone send this to?" before scripting anything. Strong opinions, relatable frustrations, and counterintuitive truths are the three most reliable share triggers in skincare. Build the tag or forward into the content itself — don't rely on a CTA to do it.

The Client Text Screenshot
SharesRetention Pattern Break Reel · 15–20 secJune
Why this is a Pattern Break: Client testimonials in skincare almost always appear as formal before/afters or written reviews. Nobody in the skincare space has used real client text message screenshots as a Reel format — yet text screenshots are one of the most viral content types on Instagram across finance, fitness, and service businesses. The format works because it's unfiltered and specific: real words, real reactions, no production layer between the audience and the result.

Execution: Sylvia opens with: "I got this text this morning." Camera cuts to a text screenshot from a real client reacting to their first week on the Brightening Peel Pads — raw, real, specific (something like "I can already see my skin is smoother, is that normal??"). Sylvia reacts, explains why what they're experiencing is exactly right, ties it to the June Reset theme. No formal CTA needed — the realness of the text is the share trigger. Women in SBB's audience will forward this to a friend who's been asking about peels or considering a skin reset. This can become a recurring series across Q2 — different client texts, different products, same format. Action item: Riley should collect real client texts with permission before June.
