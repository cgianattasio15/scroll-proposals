The 5-Point Audit

Where most real estate
social media breaks down.

Run through this checklist. Every item marked with an ✕ is a gap that's costing you reach, trust, and leads — regardless of how much content you're posting.

A defined content strategy with clear goals
Not "post 3x a week" — a real strategy with IVP, content pillars, and funnel mapping. Presence without purpose doesn't convert. Posting consistently to the wrong audience with the wrong message is just noise.
Most common gap
A content funnel (TOFU → MOFU → BOFU)
Every post should have a job. Awareness content gets you discovered. Education content builds trust. Conversion content drives action. Without a funnel, you're asking strangers to hire you before they know who you are.
Critical gap
Social SEO — keywords, captions, and discoverability
Instagram is a search engine. Captions, hashtags, alt text, and account keywords all tell the algorithm who your content is for. Without Social SEO, your content only reaches people who already follow you — not the new buyers and sellers you need.
Fixable fast
A clear IVP — what makes you different from every other agent
Every Cincinnati agent posts listings, market updates, and "just sold" photos. If your content looks like everyone else's, the algorithm treats it like everyone else's. Your Ideal Value Proposition needs to be the backbone of every piece of content you create.
Differentiator gap
Consistent posting cadence — minimum 2–3x per week
The Instagram algorithm rewards consistency above almost everything else. Posting once a week or going dark for two weeks resets your distribution. The accounts that win are the ones that show up on a schedule and let the data drive the content decisions.
Systems fix
The DWELL513 opportunity
The Cincinnati real estate market is competitive — but most agents are posting without any of the above. The opportunity isn't to outpost them. It's to out-strategize them. One account with a clear IVP, a real funnel, and consistent execution will outperform ten accounts posting randomly every time.
02
The Content Funnel

How to walk a stranger
to a signed client.

Social media isn't a place to sell. It's a place to earn trust at scale. Every piece of content maps to one of three stages — and together they create a system that turns cold audiences into warm leads without a single cold call.

TOFU
Top of Funnel
Awareness & Discovery
What it does
Gets you in front of people who don't know you yet. The goal isn't to sell — it's to be discovered by buyers and sellers in Cincinnati who are actively looking for an agent they can trust.
For DWELL513
"5 Cincinnati neighborhoods most buyers overlook in 2026" — broad reach, local authority, no ask.
MOFU
Middle of Funnel
Education & Trust
What it does
Turns awareness into credibility. This is where 10+ years of market expertise becomes your biggest asset. Educational content, market insights, and process transparency build the trust that makes someone choose you over the next agent.
For DWELL513
"What I tell every first-time buyer before they make an offer" — positions Doug as the expert, not the salesperson.
BOFU
Bottom of Funnel
Action & Lead Gen
What it does
Converts trust into conversations. By the time someone reaches this stage, they already know you, like you, and trust you. The ask becomes natural — the logical next step for someone who's been following your content.
For DWELL513
"Thinking about buying or selling in 2026? Here's where to start." — direct CTA to a warm, already-interested audience.
The key insight
Most agents skip straight to BOFU. They post listings and "just sold" content and wonder why no one reaches out. That's like asking someone to marry you on the first date. The funnel works because it earns the ask before it makes it.
