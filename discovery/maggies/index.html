Quick Wins

Five things you can do
this week.

These don't require a big budget, a content shoot, or a strategy overhaul. They're fast, high-impact moves that will immediately improve how Maggie's shows up on Instagram.

⚡
Why Start Here
Small changes to your profile and captions can dramatically change how many people find you — and what they do when they get there.
Instagram's algorithm surfaces content to people who aren't following you yet. Most small businesses are invisible to that audience not because their content is bad — but because their profile and captions aren't set up to be found. These five fixes change that.
01
Switch your account to a Professional (Creator or Business) account
If you haven't already, this is the single most important account-level change you can make. It unlocks Instagram Insights (so you can see what's actually working), adds a contact button to your profile, and tells the algorithm you're a real business — not a personal account. Takes about 60 seconds to do.
How: Go to Settings → Account → Switch to Professional Account → Select "Business" → Choose your category (Food & Beverage or Bakery)
⚡ 2 minutes
02
Rewrite your Instagram bio with keywords people actually search
Instagram is a search engine. When someone in Cincinnati types "mini cheesecakes" or "dessert catering" into the search bar, your account should show up. Right now, most bios are written for people who already know the brand — not for people discovering it for the first time. Your bio needs to tell Instagram (and new visitors) exactly what you do, who you serve, and where you are.
Try this bio structure:
Line 1: What you make — "Artisan mini cheesecakes, made from scratch"
Line 2: Who you serve — "Corporate events · Weddings · Gifting"
Line 3: Location + action — "Blue Ash, OH · Order below ↓"
⚡ 10 minutes
03
Add a keyword to your Instagram display name — not just your handle
Most people don't know this: Instagram's search algorithm uses your display name (the bold name at the top of your profile) as a searchable keyword field — separate from your handle. If your display name is just "Maggie's Marvelous Mini Cheesecakes," you're only searchable by brand name. Add a keyword and you become searchable by what you do.
Example: Change display name from "Maggie's Marvelous Mini Cheesecakes" → "Maggie's Mini Cheesecakes | Cincinnati Dessert Catering"
⚡ 2 minutes
04
Start adding on-screen text to your videos — even just one line
Instagram Reels are often watched on mute. If your video doesn't have text on screen, a large portion of viewers will scroll past without ever understanding what they're looking at. You don't need fancy editing — even a simple text overlay that says what's happening ("Making 200 mini cheesecakes for a corporate event") dramatically increases watch time and saves. This is the fastest way to make existing content perform better without reshooting anything.
Simple text ideas to add: "This is how we make [X]" · "Watch until the end" · "Mini cheesecakes for [occasion]" · "Cincinnati's best kept dessert secret"
⚡ 5 minutes per video
05
Add a comment trigger CTA to your next three posts
A comment trigger is a simple line in your caption that tells viewers to comment a specific word to get something useful sent to their DMs. It does two things at once: it drives comments (which the algorithm rewards by showing your post to more people) and it captures warm leads directly in your inbox. You don't need automation software to start — you can reply manually to begin with.
Try one of these in your next caption:
"Comment MENU and I'll send you our full flavor list"
"Comment BOOK and I'll send you our catering availability"
"Comment GIFT and I'll send you our corporate gifting options"
⚡ 2 minutes per post
What We See

Facebook vs. Instagram —
two different jobs.

You're right that Facebook is working. Here's why — and why Instagram needs a completely different approach to do the same thing.

Facebook
~2,935 page likes
Working Well
  • ✅Your existing audience already knows and trusts you here — that's genuinely valuable and worth maintaining
  • ✅Facebook is a retention platform — it keeps your current community engaged and informed
  • ✅Great for event announcements, local community posts, and word-of-mouth amplification
  • ⚠️Facebook's organic reach to new audiences has declined significantly — it's harder to grow from scratch here now
  • ⚠️The average Facebook user is older — Instagram is where the event planners, corporate buyers, and brides you want to reach are spending their time
Instagram
@maggiesminicheesecakes · ~345 followers
Big Opportunity
  • 🎯Instagram is a discovery platform — it actively shows your content to people who don't follow you yet, based on what they're interested in
  • 🎯Event planners, corporate buyers, brides, and local foodies are all on Instagram — and they use it to find vendors
  • 🎯Reels can reach thousands of new people in your area with zero ad spend — no other platform offers that for free right now
  • 📌The gap between your Facebook presence (2,935 likes) and Instagram (345 followers) is the opportunity — the audience is there, the content system just needs to be built
  • 📌Strategy: keep doing what's working on Facebook, and build Instagram as your new audience acquisition engine
Observation 01
203 posts and 345 followers tells us the content isn't reaching new people — yet.
That's not a reflection of the product quality — it's a signal that the content isn't optimized for discovery. When you post without keywords in captions, without on-screen text in videos, and without a clear CTA, Instagram doesn't know who to show it to. The fix isn't posting more — it's posting smarter. The Quick Wins above are the starting point.
Observation 02
Your highest-value customers — corporate buyers and event planners — aren't seeing proof that you can handle their order.
Corporate catering and event orders are the highest-margin, highest-volume business Maggie's can take on. But a buyer evaluating a catering vendor needs to see scale — photos and videos of large orders being prepped, packaged, and set up. That proof content is the single most powerful trust signal for a high-value buyer. It's the content that converts someone from "interested" to "let me reach out." Right now, that content doesn't exist on Instagram.
Observation 03
The "behind the bake" is your most powerful untapped content — and it costs nothing to capture.
Process content — the crust being pressed, the filling being piped, the flavors being finished, the boxes being packed — is the content that builds desire before a customer ever places an order. It's the content that gets saved, shared, and watched multiple times. In the specialty food space, process Reels consistently outperform product photos by 3–5x in reach. The best part: you're already doing all of this. You just need to start filming it.
What's Possible

Three content pillars that
drive real results for Maggie's.

This is the content framework we'd build around. Each pillar serves a different stage of the buyer journey — from first discovery all the way to a catering inquiry.

The Core Opportunity
Most specialty food brands post product photos and hope for the best. The ones that break out do three things: show the process, prove the scale, and make it easy to order.
Maggie's has an exceptional product, a genuine founder story, and a high-value revenue stream in corporate catering that nobody else in Cincinnati is owning on social media. The opportunity isn't just more followers — it's building a content system that turns Instagram into a consistent source of catering inquiries.
🎬
Pillar 01 · Discovery
Process Content
The "behind the bake" — filmed while you're already making the product. This is the content that reaches new audiences, builds desire, and earns saves and shares. No extra setup required.
  • Crust being pressed into the mold — close-up, satisfying, shareable
  • Filling being piped — slow pour, flavor reveal, visual payoff
  • "How we make 200 mini cheesecakes for a corporate event" — start to finish
  • Flavor development — "We tested 6 versions before this one made the menu"
🏢
Pillar 02 · Trust
Corporate & Event Proof
Photos and videos of large orders going out the door. This is the content that converts high-value buyers — event planners and corporate buyers need to see you can handle their scale before they'll reach out.
  • "400 minis for a corporate holiday party — here's how we pulled it off"
  • Final dessert table setup at a venue — wide shot + close-up detail
  • Packaging and delivery prep — the logistics proof that builds confidence
  • Client reaction or thank-you — social proof that compounds over time
🎁
Pillar 03 · Conversion
Occasion & Gifting Content
Content mapped to the moments when people are actively looking for what Maggie's offers — holidays, weddings, corporate gifting season. This is the content that drives direct orders and inquiries.
  • "Holiday gifting is open — here's how to order for your office"
  • Wedding dessert table inspo featuring Maggie's as the centerpiece
  • Valentine's / Mother's Day / Christmas seasonal drops with clear CTAs
  • "Planning a baby shower in Cincinnati? Here's what 50 guests looks like"

Applied to Maggie's

  • Add one line of on-screen text: "This is how we make every single one"
  • No fancy editing needed — a phone propped up on the counter is enough to start
Comment Trigger / Lead Capture
Why it works: one post does two jobs
The comment trigger ("Comment RECIPE and I'll send it to you") drives comment volume — which the algorithm rewards by showing the post to more people — while simultaneously capturing warm leads in DMs. One post, two outcomes.
Applied to Maggie's
  • "Comment MENU and I'll send you our full flavor list" — every post becomes a lead tool
  • "Comment BOOK and I'll send you our catering availability" — direct inquiry capture
  • Start by replying manually — you don't need automation software to make this work

Brand Voice / Discovery
Why it works: people buy the person, not just the product
Founder-voice content — direct, honest, personality-forward — builds the emotional connection that turns a one-time viewer into a loyal customer. People don't just want to buy cheesecakes. They want to buy from Maggie.
Applied to Maggie's
  • Maggie on camera: "I started making these in my kitchen. Now I'm delivering them to corporate events. Here's what changed."
  • The founder story — the disability, the business partner, the craft — is a story people connect with and share
  • You don't need to share everything — just enough to make it feel real and human
Social Proof / Scale
Why it works: specific numbers build instant credibility
Specific numbers — "19.3M views," "400 minis," "sold out in 3 hours" — are the social proof that validates a buyer's decision. Vague claims don't convert. Specific proof does. When you show scale, buyers believe you can handle their order.
Applied to Maggie's
  • "We just delivered 400 mini cheesecakes to a corporate holiday party. Here's what that looked like." — film it next time it happens
  • "3 flavors. 200 guests. 1 wedding. Here's how we did it." — specific + visual = the most trusted content format
  • Every large order is a content opportunity — start documenting them
