Section 01 · Profile Audit

Your profile is the
first impression.

Before a single post gets seen, Instagram's algorithm and new visitors are both evaluating your profile. Here's how Maggie's stacks up across the six elements that matter most.

Why This Matters
Your profile is a search result before it's a storefront.
Instagram's search algorithm reads your display name and bio to decide who to show your account to. A new visitor decides whether to follow in under 7 seconds. If your profile isn't set up correctly, you're invisible to new buyers — no matter how good the product is or how often you post.
Business Account
Needs Fix
If you're still on a personal account, you're missing Instagram Insights (which shows you what's actually working), the contact button, and key algorithm signals that tell Instagram you're a real business.
Action: Settings → Account → Switch to Professional Account → Business → Category: Bakery
Display Name (Search Field)
Missing
The display name is Instagram's most underused SEO field. It's searchable — separate from your handle. Right now, Maggie's is only findable by people who already know the brand name.
Action: Add a keyword-rich display name so new buyers can find you.
Copy & Paste — Suggested Display Name
Maggie's Mini Cheesecakes | Cincinnati Dessert Catering
Bio
Needs Fix
A strong bio does three things: tells Instagram what you do (for search), tells new visitors who you serve, and gives them a clear next step. Most bios are written for people who already know the brand — not for a first-time visitor or the algorithm.
Action: Rewrite the bio with keywords, audience signals, and a CTA.
Copy & Paste — Suggested Bio (150 char max)
Artisan mini cheesecakes, made from scratch Corporate events · Weddings · Gifting Blue Ash, OH · Order below ↓
Link in Bio
Needs Fix
The link in bio is the only clickable link on Instagram. It should go somewhere useful — an order form, a catering inquiry page, or a simple landing page. A generic website homepage with no clear action is a missed conversion opportunity.
Action: Link directly to your order form or a "Book a Catering Order" page. If you don't have one, even a Google Form works to start capturing inquiries.
Story Highlights
Missing
Highlights act as a permanent mini-website on your profile. New visitors use them to understand what you offer before they decide to follow or reach out. Without them, your profile has no persistent navigation.
Action: Create 4 highlights: Menu (your flavors), Events (catering proof), Order (how to book), About (Maggie's story).
Pinned Posts
Missing
Instagram lets you pin 3 posts to the top of your grid. These should be your most important, evergreen posts — not just your most recent ones. Right now, the first impression of the feed is whatever was posted last.
Action: Pin these three: (1) a process Reel showing the craft, (2) a large order / corporate event post, (3) a flavor showcase or menu post. These three tell the full story of Maggie's at a glance.
Section 02 · Content Audit

203 posts. 345 followers.
Here's why.

The posting history is there. The product is exceptional. The gap is in how the content is set up to be found, trusted, and acted on. Three observations — each with a specific fix.

01
The content isn't optimized for discovery — Instagram doesn't know who to show it to.
When captions don't include location keywords, category terms, or niche phrases, Instagram's algorithm has no context for who should see the post. It's not a content quality problem — it's a distribution setup problem. The fix is simple: add a keyword bank to every caption going forward.
Copy & Paste — Add These Keywords to Every Caption
Cincinnati · Blue Ash · mini cheesecakes · dessert catering Cincinnati · corporate catering Cincinnati · wedding desserts Cincinnati · custom cheesecakes Ohio · artisan cheesecakes · dessert table Cincinnati · specialty desserts Ohio
02
Your highest-value buyers — corporate clients and event planners — aren't seeing proof that you can handle their order.
Corporate catering is the highest-margin, highest-volume revenue stream Maggie's can build. But a buyer evaluating a catering vendor needs to see scale — photos and videos of large orders being prepped, packaged, and delivered. That proof content is the single most powerful trust signal for a high-value buyer. Right now, it's absent from the feed.
What to Film Next Time You Have a Large Order
1. The full batch laid out before packaging — wide shot, shows scale 2. The packaging process — hands placing each one into the box 3. The finished order ready to go out the door 4. Optional: the dessert table setup at the venue Caption formula: "[Number] mini cheesecakes for [event type] in [city]. Here's what that looks like."
03
Posts don't have a clear next step — so viewers watch, enjoy, and leave without doing anything.
Every post needs one specific, low-friction CTA. The most effective format on Instagram right now is the comment trigger — asking viewers to comment a word to receive something useful in their DMs. It drives comments (which the algorithm rewards) and captures warm leads directly in your inbox. You can start by replying manually — no software needed.
Three Comment Triggers to Start Using Now
Comment MENU and I'll send you our full flavor list. Comment BOOK and I'll send you our catering availability. Comment GIFT and I'll send you our corporate gifting options.

Section 04 · What's Possible

The content system that
turns Instagram into a lead source.

Once the profile is set up correctly, this is the content framework that builds consistent reach, trust, and catering inquiries. Every example below is specific to Maggie's.

The Core Opportunity
Most specialty food brands post product photos and hope for the best. The ones that break out do three things: show the process, prove the scale, and make it easy to order.
Maggie's has an exceptional product, a genuine founder story, and a high-value revenue stream in corporate catering that nobody else in Cincinnati is owning on Instagram. The opportunity isn't just more followers — it's building a content system that turns Instagram into a consistent source of catering inquiries and event bookings.
🎬
Pillar 01 · TOFU · Discovery
Process Content
The "behind the bake" — filmed while you're already making the product. This reaches new audiences, builds desire, and earns saves and shares.
  • Crust press — close-up, slow, satisfying, 15 seconds
  • Filling pour — slow, visual payoff, trending audio
  • "How we make 200 mini cheesecakes for a corporate event"
  • "We tested 6 flavors before this one made the menu"
🏢
Pillar 02 · MOFU · Trust
Corporate & Event Proof
Photos and videos of large orders going out the door. This converts high-value buyers — event planners need to see scale before they reach out.
  • "400 minis for a corporate holiday party — here's how we pulled it off"
  • Final dessert table setup at a venue — wide + detail shots
  • Packaging and delivery prep — the logistics proof
  • Client reaction or thank-you message — social proof
🎁
Pillar 03 · BOFU · Conversion
Occasion & Gifting
Content mapped to moments when people are actively looking for what Maggie's offers — holidays, weddings, corporate gifting season.
  • "Holiday gifting is open — here's how to order for your office"
  • Wedding dessert table with Maggie's as the centerpiece
  • Valentine's / Mother's Day / Christmas seasonal drops with CTAs
  • "Planning a baby shower in Cincinnati? Here's what 50 guests looks like"

Process Content · TOFU
The process IS the hook
This Reel shows the craft in detail — ingredients, steps, final result. The viewer is sold on the product before they ever see a price or CTA. Process content builds desire before the order exists.
Applied to Maggie's
  • Film the crust press, the filling pipe, the flavor finish — let the process be the entire video
  • Add one line of on-screen text: "This is how we make every single one"
  • A phone propped on the counter is enough — no fancy equipment needed
