## Payment Policy
Scroll Media's standard billing process is automatic payment. The first invoice sent under this Agreement will allow Client to add a payment method through Scroll Media's Stripe billing system. Once that payment method is added, Client authorizes Scroll Media to automatically charge that saved method for future invoices, recurring retainers, prorated amounts, approved add-ons, and any other charges due under this Agreement.

All invoices are due upon receipt unless the Parties agree otherwise in writing. The recurring execution portion of this engagement is typically billed every fifteen (15) days in equal installment amounts unless the Parties agree to a different billing cadence in writing. If a payment method fails or becomes invalid, Client agrees to update it promptly so services and publishing are not disrupted. Any manual payment exception must be approved by Scroll Media in advance and may be revoked at any time.

## Client Responsibilities
Client agrees to support the engagement in a timely and collaborative way. This includes timely approvals, consolidated feedback, accurate business information, required account access, necessary brand assets, and reasonable availability for shoots, meetings, and reviews. Client is responsible for making sure any claims, offers, pricing, promotions, testimonials, and business details provided to Scroll Media are accurate and approved for use.

Client will maintain one main point of contact for approvals and strategic communication unless the Parties agree otherwise in writing.

## Content Production and Shoot Policy
Shoot frequency is tied to the active package. For this engagement, content shoots are planned according to the cadence listed in the Services section unless the Parties agree to a custom production cadence in writing.

Scroll Media will send a shot list before each scheduled shoot. Client will submit any shot-list feedback before the lock date communicated by Scroll Media. Once a shot list is locked, no material changes or net-new concepts will be added to that shoot. Minor substitutions within an existing planned shot category may be accommodated at Scroll Media's discretion.

If Client requests day-of changes that cause Scroll Media to move away from the locked shot list, Scroll Media is not responsible for any resulting content gap, delay, missed posting date, or reduced content volume.

Any extra shoot outside the standard cadence is additional scope and may be quoted separately.

## Approvals and Reviews
Scroll Media manages content reviews in the designated approval platform, currently Plann, or another platform designated by Scroll Media with reasonable notice. Content is typically submitted at least five (5) business days before the first scheduled publish date in a batch.

Client agrees to review submitted content and submit all feedback, revision requests, and approvals only through the designated approval platform. Feedback sent by text, email, phone, or any other channel does not count as an official revision request or approval under this Agreement.

Client will review content within two (2) business days after submission. To protect the posting schedule, final requested changes must be submitted before the content lock communicated by Scroll Media and no later than forty-eight (48) hours before the scheduled publish time unless Scroll Media agrees otherwise in writing.

If approvals, feedback, assets, or answers are delayed, Scroll Media is not responsible for resulting posting delays, missed dates, content-calendar changes, or related performance impact.

## Revisions Policy
Scroll Media includes reasonable revisions so the content reflects the approved strategy and brand direction. During the first thirty (30) days of live content delivery, revisions may be handled more flexibly so both Parties can calibrate creative direction, messaging, and execution style. During the strategy phase, Scroll Media will also work in good faith with Client to finalize strategic direction before execution begins.

After the initial calibration period, each content batch includes up to two (2) rounds of revisions unless the Parties agree otherwise in writing. A revision round means one consolidated set of feedback from the designated Client contact. Revision requests submitted after the review window, after content lock, or after approval may be applied to the next cycle instead of the current one.

Requests that materially change the approved strategy, require new production, create additional deliverables, expand the active channel scope, or otherwise go beyond the agreed package may be treated as additional scope.

## Instagram Stories Policy
Scroll Media leads the strategy and execution of Instagram Stories. Stories are not subject to the same approval workflow as feed posts.

Client's input on Stories is limited to sharing promotional priorities, including events, offers, product or service features, and other time-sensitive updates. Scroll Media will use reasonable efforts to include those priorities in the Stories plan, but placement, format, timing, and frequency remain at Scroll Media's discretion.

During the early calibration period, Scroll Media may collaborate more closely with Client on Stories tone and style. After that period, Scroll Media may execute Stories independently based on the preferences established during the engagement.

If Client identifies a factual error in Stories content, Client must flag it as early as reasonably possible. Scroll Media will use commercially reasonable efforts to correct flagged factual errors before publish when timing allows.

## Client Communication and Reporting
Scroll Media will hold one (1) biweekly check-in meeting during active execution. Meetings are generally thirty (30) minutes and are used to cover content approvals, shot-list alignment, performance signals, priorities, and Stories promotional input.

Scroll Media will deliver a monthly performance report during the first week of each calendar month covering the prior month's performance. Reports are delivered through a shared report link with a brief summary email. No separate report-review meeting is required unless the Parties agree otherwise in writing.

The current client-facing policy guide that explains Scroll Media's operating process in more detail is available at: <https://tools.scrollmedia.co/policies/content-review/>.

That guide is intended to explain the workflow summarized in this Agreement. If there is any conflict between that guide and this Agreement, this Agreement controls.

## Termination
Either Party may terminate this Agreement if the other Party materially breaches the Agreement and does not cure that breach within a commercially reasonable period after written notice.

After services have begun, either Party may also terminate this Agreement for convenience by providing at least fourteen (14) days' prior written notice.

If Client terminates this Agreement early for convenience after work has begun, Client remains responsible for all amounts already billed, all approved non-cancellable costs, and the value of work already performed or committed through the effective termination date. If Scroll Media cannot or will not perform its obligations in any material respect under this Agreement, Scroll Media will promptly notify Client in writing, and Client will not be responsible for paying for services not yet performed.
