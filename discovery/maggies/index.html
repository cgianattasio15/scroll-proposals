Why Start Here
Most small businesses aren't invisible on Instagram because their content is bad. They're invisible because their profile and captions aren't set up to be found.
Instagram is a search engine. Every one of these fixes tells the algorithm exactly who you are, who you serve, and where you are — so it can start showing your content to the right people. These five changes take less than an hour total and will immediately improve how Maggie's shows up.
01
Switch to a Professional (Business) Account
If you haven't already, this is the single most important account-level change you can make. It unlocks Instagram Insights (so you can see what's actually working), adds a contact button to your profile, and signals to the algorithm that you're a real business. Takes about 60 seconds.
Step-by-Step
Settings → Account → Switch to Professional Account → Select "Business" → Category: Bakery (or Food & Beverage) → Add your email and phone number → Done.
⚡ 2 minutes
02
Add a keyword to your Instagram display name
Most people don't know this: Instagram's search algorithm uses your display name (the bold name at the top of your profile) as a searchable keyword field — separate from your handle. If your display name is just your brand name, you're only searchable by people who already know you. Add a keyword and you become searchable by what you do and where you do it.
Copy & Paste — Suggested Display Name
Maggie's Mini Cheesecakes | Cincinnati Dessert Catering
Why It Works
When someone in Cincinnati searches "dessert catering" or "mini cheesecakes" in Instagram's search bar, this display name makes Maggie's show up. The old display name only showed up for people searching the exact brand name.
⚡ 2 minutes
03
Rewrite your Instagram bio with keywords people actually search
Your bio needs to do three things: tell Instagram what you do, tell new visitors who you serve, and give them a clear next step. Most bios are written for people who already know the brand. This one is written for someone discovering Maggie's for the first time — and for the algorithm that decides who to show it to.
Copy & Paste — Suggested Bio (150 characters max)
Artisan mini cheesecakes, made from scratch Corporate events · Weddings · Gifting Blue Ash, OH · Order below ↓
What Each Line Does
Line 1: What you make — keyword-rich, specific, search-friendly Line 2: Who you serve — signals to corporate buyers, brides, and gifters that this is for them Line 3: Location + action — tells the algorithm where you are and tells visitors what to do next
⚡ 10 minutes
04
Add one line of on-screen text to your next video
Instagram Reels are watched on mute by a large portion of viewers. If your video doesn't have text on screen, those viewers scroll past without ever knowing what they're looking at. You don't need fancy editing — even one line of text that says what's happening dramatically increases watch time and saves. Here are five lines you can use on your next five videos, ready to go.
Copy & Paste — 5 On-Screen Text Lines (one per video)
Video 1: "This is how we make every single one from scratch" Video 2: "200 mini cheesecakes for a corporate event — watch" Video 3: "Cincinnati's best kept dessert secret" Video 4: "The filling takes 3 ingredients. Here's what happens next" Video 5: "We've been making these in Blue Ash for [X] years"
⚡ 5 minutes per video
05
Add a comment trigger CTA to your next three posts
A comment trigger is a line in your caption that asks viewers to comment a specific word to get something useful sent to their DMs. It does two things at once: it drives comments (which the algorithm rewards by showing your post to more people) and it captures warm leads directly in your inbox. You can start by replying manually — no automation software needed. Here are three ready-to-use captions for your next three posts.
Copy & Paste — Caption 1 (Process / TOFU)
Every single one of these is made from scratch in Blue Ash, Ohio. No shortcuts. No pre-made bases. Just real ingredients, real craft, and a lot of cheesecake. We've been doing this for [X] years — and the process hasn't changed. Comment MENU below and I'll send you our full flavor list. ↓
Copy & Paste — Caption 2 (Corporate / MOFU)
Planning a corporate event in Cincinnati? We handle everything from 50 to 500+ guests — individually portioned, beautifully packaged, and ready to impress. No messy slices. No serving drama. Just mini cheesecakes that people actually remember. Comment BOOK below and I'll send you our catering availability for [month]. ↓
Copy & Paste — Caption 3 (Gifting / BOFU)
The best corporate gift in Cincinnati isn't a gift card. It's a box of mini cheesecakes from Maggie's — custom flavors, branded packaging, delivered to your office. We're currently taking orders for [holiday/season]. Comment GIFT below and I'll send you our corporate gifting options. ↓
⚡ 5 minutes per post
What We See

Facebook vs. Instagram —
two different jobs.

You're right that Facebook is working. Here's why — and why Instagram needs a completely different approach to do the same thing.

Facebook
~2,935 page likes
Working Well
  • ✓Your existing audience already knows and trusts you here — that's genuinely valuable and worth maintaining
  • ✓Facebook is a retention platform — it keeps your current community engaged and informed
  • ✓Great for event announcements, local community posts, and word-of-mouth amplification
  • ⚠Facebook's organic reach to new audiences has declined significantly — it's harder to grow from scratch here now
  • ⚠The event planners, corporate buyers, and brides you want to reach are spending more time on Instagram than Facebook
Instagram
@maggiesminicheesecakes · ~345 followers
Big Opportunity
  • 🎯Instagram is a discovery platform — it actively shows your content to people who don't follow you yet, based on what they're interested in
  • 🎯Event planners, corporate buyers, brides, and local foodies all use Instagram to find vendors before they ever Google anything
  • 🎯Reels can reach thousands of new people in your area with zero ad spend — no other platform offers that organically right now
  • 📌The gap between your Facebook presence (2,935 likes) and Instagram (345 followers) is the opportunity — the audience is there, the content system just needs to be built
  • 📌Strategy: keep doing what's working on Facebook, and build Instagram as your new audience acquisition engine
Observation 01
203 posts and 345 followers tells us the content isn't reaching new people — yet.
That's not a reflection of the product quality — it's a signal that the content isn't optimized for discovery. When you post without keywords in captions, without on-screen text in videos, and without a clear CTA, Instagram doesn't know who to show it to. The fix isn't posting more — it's posting smarter. The Quick Wins above are the starting point.
The Fix — Add These Keywords to Every Caption
Cincinnati · Blue Ash · mini cheesecakes · dessert catering · corporate catering Cincinnati · wedding desserts Cincinnati · custom cheesecakes Ohio · artisan cheesecakes · dessert table Cincinnati
Observation 02
Your highest-value customers — corporate buyers and event planners — aren't seeing proof that you can handle their order.
Corporate catering and event orders are the highest-margin, highest-volume business Maggie's can take on. But a buyer evaluating a catering vendor needs to see scale — photos and videos of large orders being prepped, packaged, and set up. That proof content is the single most powerful trust signal for a high-value buyer. It converts someone from "interested" to "let me reach out." Right now, that content doesn't exist on Instagram.
What to Film Next Time You Have a Large Order
1. The full batch laid out before packaging (wide shot — shows scale) 2. The packaging process — hands placing each one into the box 3. The final packaged order ready to go out the door 4. Optional: a quick 5-second clip of the delivery setup at the venue Caption formula: "[Number] mini cheesecakes for [event type] in [city]. Here's what that looks like."
Observation 03
The "behind the bake" is your most powerful untapped content — and it costs nothing to capture.
Process content — the crust being pressed, the filling being piped, the flavors being finished, the boxes being packed — is the content that builds desire before a customer ever places an order. It gets saved, shared, and watched multiple times. In the specialty food space, process Reels consistently outperform product photos by 3–5x in reach. You're already doing all of this. You just need to start filming it.
5 Process Moments to Film This Week (phone propped on counter is enough)
1. Pressing the crust into the mold — close-up, slow, satisfying 2. Pouring or piping the filling — the pour is the hook 3. Adding the topping or flavor finish — the reveal moment 4. Pulling a finished tray out of the fridge — the payoff 5. Cutting or plating a finished cheesecake — the money shot

Most specialty food brands post product photos and hope for the best. The ones that break out do three things: show the process, prove the scale, and make it easy to order.
