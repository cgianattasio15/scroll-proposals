Foundation

What makes Atlas different.

Social is crowded, and "another tech consultant" doesn't stand out. Atlas wins by leaning into what's genuinely unique. These four differentiators are the triggers we weave into every piece of content.

Experience10+ years in regulated enterprise (healthcare, fintech, data), where quality and security actually matter, now applied to small business.Feeds content: "how I'd fix X" teardowns, enterprise lessons made small-business-simple.
ContinuitySenior accountability backed by a vetted team, built to last. Not a one-person gamble.Feeds content: the founder story, the team behind him, the "still here in two years" answer.
ClarityFixed price, written scope. A number you can plan around, no open-ended "it depends."Feeds content: demystifying cost and scope, the Technology Assessment walkthrough.
No hypeGrounded AI. The calm translator who tells you what's real for a business your size.Feeds content: anti-hype takes, "you don't need AI, you need this one thing."
The objection we counter-position against

"Can a young, founder-led shop carry this, and still be here in two years?"

Every competitor is a faceless agency or a hype merchant. Atlas takes the opposite position: a named, senior operator with a real track record and a team behind him. These differentiators answer the question before the viewer asks it, and they are also the content.

07
Why organic social

Over 70% of the world is on social media for an average of 2.5 hours per day. We've never had this level of consumer attention in a single marketing channel. Our strategy captures that attention for Atlas, and gets you in front of the audience that cares most about your product. When you do that well, qualified leads follow. You can't grow a business without people knowing about you. Organic social is where they're paying attention.

The PlanReaching them

Start broad to get found. Narrow to get chosen.

We open wide to reach as many of the right people as possible, then compress over time to the hyper-local owner Atlas closes. We call it the accordion: wide at the top for reach, narrowing to precision as audience quality climbs.

The accordion

WidestBroad reach · burst into the feed"AI for small business" · "small business automation" · "AI tools for business"
NarrowThe right category · service businesses"AI for service businesses" · "automation for professional services" · "business automation consultant"
LocalThe geography · Cincinnati and KY"Cincinnati small business tech" · "Northern Kentucky IT consultant" · "Cincinnati business automation"
Hyper-localThe exact buyer · the operator we close"fractional CTO Cincinnati" · "Florence KY tech consultant" · "NKY AI consultant"
Month 1: reach (wide audience, just earning attention) to Month 6+: precision (narrow, high-quality, ready to act). The audience narrows while quality rises at every step, mirroring the funnel's shape.

Performance

How we measure, and what good looks like.

Followers and views are context, not the scoreboard. Here's exactly what we track at each stage, the target range for where you are today, and how it shows up in your monthly report.

Top of funnel
  • Views · 10,000–40,000/mo
  • Shares · 15–60
  • New followers · 40–90
  • Is the right audience finding us?
Middle of funnel
  • Profile visits · 50–500
  • Retention % · 35–50%
  • Saves · 20–80
  • Are they coming to trust the founder?
Bottom of funnel
  • Link taps · 5–40
  • Conversion rate · CTR 3–8%
  • Are they taking action? Booked fit calls are the north star we're driving toward, not yet wired into reporting.
These are Spark-stage ranges, where a brand-new account lands. As Atlas climbs to Lift and Rise, the targets rise with it. The stage sets the benchmark so we measure against where you actually are, not an account three years ahead.
Your monthly report. The first week of every month, for the month prior, you get a scored report against these three goals, your top posts, and the focus for next month. Plus a bi-weekly check-in and a quarterly re-baseline.
The conversion lens

Right audience. Clear path. The right metrics. Get Atlas in front of the right owners. Walk them through a clear path to a fit call. Track the metrics aligned to that path. With those three working, qualified lead generation follows. Hard last-mile attribution isn't running yet. We track signal first, attribution-grade second.

Appendix

Posting cadence, days, and timing.

The reference behind our cadence. These are starting defaults we calibrate to Atlas's own data once posts are live.

How often

3 feed posts + 2 story days

Monday, Wednesday, Friday on the feed, one per funnel stage, with Tuesday and Thursday for stories and reactive moments. Consistency beats volume.

What days

Weekdays for a B2B owner

Your buyer is an operator checking their phone between meetings. Tuesday through Thursday tend to perform best for reach to business owners. We confirm against real data.

What time

Early morning and lunch

Around 7 to 9am and the midday break are when this audience scrolls. We post ahead of those windows and adjust to Atlas's actual top times.

Format mix

40 / 35 / 25

Roughly 40% top of funnel, 35% middle, 25% bottom. Read monthly, not post by post. Reels for reach, carousels for saves, stories for conversion.

Engagement

About 30 minutes a day

Manual engagement with the right local accounts, and replying to every comment and DM. The algorithm rewards it and your buyer notices it.

Distribution

Cross-post to LinkedIn

The founder's strongest content also runs on LinkedIn 3 to 5 times a week, where many of these owners also spend time.
